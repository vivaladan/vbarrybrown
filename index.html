Vikki Barry Brown

Hello, I’m Vikki, welcome to my site! I am a Leverhulme Doctoral Candidate in the School of Geography at Queen Mary University of London (QMUL) where my supervisors are Professor Catherine Nash [https://www.qmul.ac.uk/geog/staff/nashc.html] and Dr Tim Brown [https://www.qmul.ac.uk/geog/staff/brownt.html]. My PhD research ‘Being English in Ireland – English migration to Ireland, 1960 to the present’ is a qualitative project considering English people who have moved to Ireland, finding out how they experience identity, belonging and home through in-depth interviewing. You can read more about this research at the dedicated website here [https://englishinireland.vbarrybrown.com/].

I am part of the Queen Mary University of London Leverhulme Trust Doctoral Scholarship Mobile People cohort [https://www.qmul.ac.uk/ihss/people/doctoral-students/] an interdisciplinary programme concerned with the transformative effects of mobile people on the social and political institutions they inhabit and construct.
